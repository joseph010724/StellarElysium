What Are Zodiac Signs?


Zodiac signs are a system of astrology that divides the sky into 12 segments, each associated with a specific constellation.

They influence personality traits, behaviors, and even destiny based on the position of the sun, moon, and planets at the time of a person's birth.

The zodiac has roots in Babylonian, Egyptian, and Greek traditions.

Each sign corresponds to a specific time of the year and has unique traits and ruling elements.

The zodiac is a 12-part circle (360° total), where each sign occupies 30°.


Why Are Zodiac Signs Significant in Astrology?


Astrologers believe that the zodiac signs influence:

༺☆༻ Personality Traits: Each sign has unique qualities, strengths, and weaknesses.

༺☆༻ Behavior and Preferences: Certain patterns of behavior are attributed to individuals based on their sign.

༺☆༻ Relationships: Signs are used to determine compatibility in friendships, love, and work dynamics.

Zodiac Groupings


There are four elements in Zodiac Signs:


Fire: (Aries, Leo, Sagittarius): Passionate, energetic, dynamic.

Earth: (Taurus, Virgo, Capricorn): Practical, grounded, reliable.

Air (Gemini, Libra, Aquarius): Intellectual, communicative, social.

Water (Cancer, Scorpio, Pisces): Emotional, intuitive, deep.
